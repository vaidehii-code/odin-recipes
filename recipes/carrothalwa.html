Ingredients

  • 1Nestlé MILKMAID Mini
  • 2 cups (200 gm)Flour
  • 600 gmsGrated Paneer
  • 200 gmSooji/Rava
  • 1½ tspBaking Powder
  • 1½ tspBaking Soda
  • For FryingOil
  • 2 litresWater
  • 1 kgSugar
  • 6-8 nos Coarsely Crushed Cardamom

How to make Gulab Jamun

  1. Step 1: Combine sugar and water in a pan and bring to a boil to create the syrup. Remove from heat, add cardamom, and set aside to cool.
  2. Step 2: In a mixing bowl, combine flour, paneer, sooji, Nestlé MILKMAID, baking powder, and baking soda. Mix gently to form a soft dough, avoiding over-kneading.
  3. Step 3: Divide the dough into 30-35 portions and gently shape them into round balls.
  4. Step 4: Heat oil on low flame and fry the balls until they turn golden brown.
  5. Step 5: Transfer the fried Gulab Jamuns into the cooled sugar syrup. Once all the Gulab Jamuns are added, bring the syrup to a boil again briefly, then remove from heat.
  6. Step 6: Enjoy your homemade Gulab Jamun warm, garnished with your favourite toppings.

Tips to Make Gulab Jamun

Allow the dough to rest before shaping to ensure softer Gulab jamun. Divide dough equally and shape smooth, crack-free balls with gentle handling, avoiding kneading or excessive pressure. Make small dough balls since they expand while frying and soaking in syrup. Test the oil with a small piece of dough; it should rise slowly without turning dark immediately. Fry Gulab Jamuns on a gentle flame to cook evenly without browning outside too quickly. Let fried Gulab Jamuns cool a bit before adding to warm syrup to avoid shrinking. Garnish with chopped nuts or edible silver leaf for extra flavour and elegance. As mentioned in the above Gulab Jamun recipe, serve the sweet warm or at room temperature for the best taste and texture.
